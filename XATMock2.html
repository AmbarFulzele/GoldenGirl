Last year, Illinois was one of a handful of states that lost population. Emigration became a campaign issue in the governor's race last year and has some throwing up caution flags. But the numbers don't mean there is a crisis, or even a real clamor, to leave the state. It is true Illinois i lost population overall last year. More than 90,000 people moved elsewhere. In his State of the State Address, Gov. Bruce Rauner mentioned that over the past dozen years 250,000 people picked up and moved out of Illinois. That is correct. However, people also moved into the state and were born here. With the exception of last year, population has generally grown in Illinois at a small rate. Which of the following statements, if true, best supports the author's claim that "the numbers don't mean there is a crisis"?
A. The people who shifted temporarily a few years ago to Illinois, where the effects of. recession was felt the least among all the states, have moved back to their home state last year.
B. Recession has slowed down migration across the states within the country.
C. The number of people that migrate into Illinois has reduced because of the high taxes imposed in Illinois.
D. Gov. Bruce Rauner is known to publicize obscure Statistics in a quest for gaining public confidence.
E. None of these
Status :  
Unanswered
Answer : A
The passage says that even though the population of Illinois has reduced in the past year, this does not necessarily imply that there is a crisis in the state. The reduction in the population can be due to more number of people leaving the state or less number of people moving into the state assuming birth and death rates do not change drastically.
Option A: This statement gives a possible explanation for the loss in population of the state. The ''aerial spraying of coca crops'' which the Colombian government banned would most likely result in
A. the withering of the coca crop which will result in a reduction in the export of the crop.
B. providing excess nutrients to the coca crops which will accelerate its growth.

Mark [1], if the question can be answered by using one of the statements alone, but not by the other.
Mark [2], if the question can be answered by using either of the statements alone. Mark [3], if the question can be answered by using both the statements together, but not by either statement alone.
Mark [4], if the question cannot be answered even by using both statements together.
a, b, c and d are natural numbers.
Is abc + bcd + acd even?
I: abc + bcd is even
II: bcd + acd is even
A. 1
B. 2
C. 3
D. 4
E. None of these
Status :  
Unanswered
Answer : D
Statement (I)
If abc + bcd is even, acd has to be even in order to make abc + bcd + acd even.
If abc + bcd is even, either both abc and bcd are even or both abc and bcd are odd.
If abc is even, at least one of a, b and c is even. Similarly, if bcd is even, at least one of b, c and d is even. Therefore, at least one of a, c and d must be even. Therefore, acd is even and hence the sum abc + bcd + acd is even.
If both abc and bcd are odd, all of a, b, c and d must be odd. Therefore, product acd will be odd and hence the sum abc + bcd + acd is odd.

Using similar argument as for statement (I), statement (II) alone is not sufficient to answer the question.
Combining statements (I) and (II)
